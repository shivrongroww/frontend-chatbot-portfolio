## The Problem

Describe the problem in plain language. Answer:
- What was broken, missing, or unclear?
- Who was experiencing this problem? (users, the business, both?)
- Why did it matter — what was the cost of not solving it?
- What constraints existed going in? (technical, timeline, resources)

Write 2–4 paragraphs. Be specific — vague problems make vague answers.

Example:
"Users were abandoning the checkout flow at the payment step at a 60% rate. The team
assumed it was a trust issue with the payment form, but we hadn't validated that.
We had 6 weeks and one engineer available for implementation."

---

## My Role and Responsibilities

Be explicit about what you personally did vs. what the team did. The chatbot will
speak in first person, so this section needs to be clear.

- I was responsible for...
- I led / facilitated / contributed to...
- I collaborated with [role] on...
- I was NOT responsible for... (optional, but helpful for scope clarity)

## Discovery and Research

What did you do to understand the problem before jumping to solutions?

- Methods used (user interviews, surveys, analytics, competitor audit, heuristic review, etc.)
- How many users/participants were involved
- Key things you learned that changed or confirmed your assumptions
- Any surprising findings

Example:
"I ran 6 user interviews and found that the drop-off had nothing to do with trust —
users were confused about which shipping option was pre-selected. This reframed the
entire problem."

---

## Process and Approach

Walk through how you worked, not just what you made. This is where your thinking shows.

1. **[Step / Phase name]** — What you did and why. What decisions were made here.
2. **[Step / Phase name]** — What you tried, what didn't work, what you pivoted from.
3. **[Step / Phase name]** — How you narrowed down and validated direction.

Include:
- How you explored multiple directions (ideation, sketches, competing concepts)
- Key decision points and the reasoning behind your choices
- How you involved stakeholders or users in the process
- What you cut and why

## Outcome and Impact

This is the most important section for the chatbot. Be as specific as possible.

**Quantitative results** (use real numbers if you have them, ranges if not):
- [Metric] improved from [X] to [Y] — e.g. "Drop-off reduced from 60% to 36%"
- [Metric] increased by [X]% after launch
- [Business result] — e.g. "Feature shipped to 100k users in Q3 2024"

**Qualitative results:**
- What did users say?
- What did stakeholders say?
- What changed in how the team or company operated?

**If metrics aren't available:** Describe the scope (who uses it, how often) and what you observed.

## What I Learned

2–3 things you genuinely learned on this project — about design, users, collaboration,
or yourself. Be honest. This is what makes a portfolio feel real.

Example:
"I learned that I default to solutions too quickly under time pressure. On this project,
spending an extra week in discovery saved us from building the wrong thing entirely."
